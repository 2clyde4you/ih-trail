    IGNITE VR ARCADE
    ​

    Reprsents the possible future of arcades


    ​

        However, all is not lost for the future of arcades. One of the new arcades out there was the Ignite VR Arcade, at Marina Square Malls. What makes it special is that this arcade specialises in Virtual Reality arcade games, one of the first of its kind in Singapore. It first opened in December 2016. It was founded by 28 year old Mr Ray Khoon, who started it after he got hooked onto VR Gaming after he tried it in 2015. The arcade hosts a whole variety of games, aimed at the younger audience and teens. It runs by and hourly renting service where one person can use the system and try it out. Many people have tried it and find it quite fun.  One customer, Mr Ikhsan Suri, 28, visits the arcade at least once a week with friends.

    "At first I thought it would be a bit gimmicky, but once you put on the headset, wow, it is like you are in another world. I was hooked," he said.

    Sadly though, the lease for the land where the arcade is ended in 23rd May 2017 and the owners did not wish to renew it for several reasons. Firstly they wish to improve how they operate the arcade, as during their short running period, there were many issues. In addition, they want to develop more games on their own to have the freedom over the games they display. They are likely to eventually come back though in the meantime there are still other arcades in Singapore. This is just one example of some VR arcades, and represents a possible future for arcades, a way to evolve and stay relevant. We can only wait and see what is to come for the future of arcades.

    THE FUTURE OF ARCADES
    ​

    VirtualLand arcade

    ​

    Virtualand is an arcade chain throughout singapore, which is run by a company called TKA Amusement. However, with mobile gaming on the rise, One of Virtualand’s largest outlets, Located in Bugis, has fallen due to the lack of customers.
    In the past, many people have visited Bugis’s Virtualand arcade as it was convenient and cheap. They spent time playing with their friends in the arcade, and had many unforgettable experiences. Bugis’s Virtualand arcade also was the venue of choice for many popular events, like the meet-and-greet session with fighting game Tekken’s Director Katsuhiro Harada and Tekken Project Senior Game Designer Michael Murray in 2012.    
    In order for virtualand to remain popular, they had to rent large retail spaces as well as  purchase and maintaining costs of gaming machines also posed challenges for the operators of arcades. These requirements were costly, and the retention was low as people got bored quickly.
    The future of Virtualand seems uncertain, and people suspected that this incident may be foreshadowing for what may happen in the future, which is the closing down of arcade gaming stores nationwide, as mobile gaming continues to rise in popularity.

    TIME ZONE, THE ARCADE OF THE PRESENT
    ​

    The arcade represents that arcades of the present  


               Timezone is an international chain of video game arcades that is commonly found in Singapore. Timezone is based and owned in Australia. Initially, Timezone had regular coin-operated machines just like any other arcade. However, the system was moved to a “powercard” system where the card had to be swiped to play the game. Customers were to deposit money into the card before playing and they could receive tickets which can be used to redeem items using it. There are different types of ‘powercards’ which give you different levels of privileges. In Singapore, timezone is the leading arcade chain. They also brought back physical games such as basketball shooting and bowling, unlike the arcades of the previous generation which mainly focused on video games played mainly with buttons and joysticks.

        This is the arcades that most of the current generation is familiar with. It has managed to stay strong in spite of surge in mobile gaming apps and home video game businesses. It constantly contains card game arcades to attract younger kids and Shooting Games to attract teens. This is definitely an arcade that is likely to be around for much longer.

    SUPER FUN WORLD ARCADE
    ​

    This arcade represents the arcades of the previous generation. This arcade was (probably) one of the first few arcades in Singapore, even though in a sense it is not strictly a pure arcade. The Super Fun World Arcade is actually a sub company of the SuperBowl Company, which manages bowling areas. Regardless, the SuperBowl Company is one of the oldest companies in Singapore, and likely the Super Fun World would be quite old as well. It still houses some of the oldest games that people in the past played, such as Daytona USA, a high quality racing game that was very popular with people in the past.  In addition, to the one in Jurong, there is also another arcade in Marina Square which to this day is still quite popular and usually has young adults who once played it visiting there.  

        In 1983, the arcades were actually once banned. This is because many teens of the time were getting addicted to arcade games, and as a result were playing truant or stealing money to play more games. Regardless of the ban though, many hardcore fans of arcade games would go to computer stores to rent computers to play games, though they were still different from the usual games they were used to. This likely also caused a decline in arcade games up till after the ban ended as during the ban, because this caused the home video game business to grow. Still arcades of the past are still widely treasured and are popular with young adults who once played this games when they were young.
